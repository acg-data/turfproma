What is Overseeding?

Overseeding is the process of spreading grass seed over an existing lawn to thicken turf, fill in bare or thin spots, and introduce newer, improved grass varieties. When combined with aeration, overseeding allows seed to make better contact with the soil, improving germination rates and overall results.

Thicker Turf

Fills in thin areas and bare spots, creating a denser, more uniform lawn appearance.

Improved Varieties

Introduces newer grass cultivars with better disease resistance, drought tolerance, and color.

Healthier Lawn

A thicker lawn naturally crowds out weeds and handles stress, traffic, and seasonal changes better.

Why Overseeding Matters

Lawns naturally thin over time due to age, foot traffic, weather stress, and disease pressure. Overseeding rejuvenates your lawn by adding fresh, young grass plants that bring new life and vigor to your turf.

  • Fills bare and thin spots naturally
  • Introduces disease-resistant grass varieties
  • Improves overall lawn color and density
  • Helps crowd out weeds naturally
  • Increases drought and heat tolerance

Best Time to Overseed

For cool-season grasses in Massachusetts, timing is important:

Late Summer / Early Fall

Best results - warm soil, cooler air temps

Early Spring

Secondary option if fall was missed
